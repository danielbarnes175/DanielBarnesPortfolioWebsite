My First Blog Post - On making the blog.

November 24, 2018.

Well well well, looks like I finally got around to making a blog. Well, kind of. It isn't really a blog in the sense that you might think of me going onto a website, and filling in a post writing something, much like many blog hosting services around the world. Instead for some reason, I wanted to make my life difficult and my GitHub pages allowed size fill up quite fast.

As I'm writing this I have World On Fire from Tokyo ESP playing in my right ear, and I'm sitting on a cramped bus on my way back to college from Thanksgiving break. My bus ride is from 8:15am-5:00pm, so I decided to make the best of it and create a blog.

Now you might be thinking something along the lines of "How is your blog made? You said it wasn't what I would think of earlier..." Well Jimmy, calm down, I'll get to that. Instead I think I'll tell a story about a podcast I listened to last night, so strap in James, it is going to be a ride.

I knew that I had to get up early in order to be ready for my bus. Throughout Thanksgiving break, I had been waking up at 10 or 11. I need to fix that, I know, but that isn't the point. For the bus ride I would need to wake up at 5:45 am. It was early, but y'know maybe this would be the switch that I would need to start waking up early like all the Navy Seals say you should do.

Like any normal person that has to get up early, I went to bed at a completely reasonable time. Well, at least that's what I wanted to think. It was 10pm. I was laying in bed, thinking about how I should have gone to bed an hour earlier. Oh well, the opportunity had passed. Such a shame. I finally decided to try to fall asleep, but since it was Thanksgiving break, my entire family was home.

If you've never actually met the Barnes family in person, you would probably not know that they can get very loud. Well this time was no different, and a few of my sisters were talking, and laughing, and well, I couldn't really sleep well. This brings us to the part of the story that I brought you here for: I turned on a podcast.

I decided to listen to a podcast as I would fall asleep. The podcast was by Justin Stenstrom, and the guest was Neil Pasricha. Neil had written this book titled The Happiness Equation and he was here to talk about the happiness equation, and how to stay resilient when life pushes you down.

Long story short, part of the happiness equation was finding an outlet for your thoughts. The two main outlets that Neil pushed for were reading and writing. I like reading, but I thought that writing seemed a lot more my style. I love thinking about what's going on in my life, and being able to write it down will allow me to organize my thoughts. That's something that I think is very important, and a strong argument for needing an outlet that Neil brought forth.

So that's where this blog comes into play. I'll try to write as much as I can, but I also want to keep it in moderation because I do have other priorities such as school. And since we're talking about the blog again, I can finally let you know how the blog was created.

Much like this website and its use of static pages, the blog is no different in being horrible for code reuse. Each post is a seperate file in the folder for this website on my computer. In fact, to post to the blog, I have to make a commit on Git. I'm ok with this way of doing it though. It feels mmore like my creation this way. It fits more in line with my nerd-programming self. And hey, I also get some free Git contributions on my GitHub profile just for writing. Easy way to pad my stats, don't tell the recruiters!

I initially just wanted to make this post a short test to make sure the functionality was what I wanted, but I think that this actually turned into a solid first post. Crazy how when I start writing, it always ends up being more than what I bargained for. With that said, I'm going to end it here. If you enjoyed this, check out some of my other posts.

Cheers,
Daniel Barnes
